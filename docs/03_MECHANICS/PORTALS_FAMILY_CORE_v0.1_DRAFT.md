## 1. Purpose

This document records the selected core for a small family game version of **Portals** after reviewing the visual board concept, resource economy, space types, card decks, family-game pacing, and professional studio evaluation criteria.

The goal is not to create a heavy system with dozens of rules. The goal is to build a compact product: a family adventure race through portals where every turn creates a route choice, a resource decision, a risk/reward moment, and visible progress toward the final portal.

This is a working DRAFT. It is not approved canon and not final rules.

## 2. Main product decision

Do not build only a beautiful elemental map. Build a compact family game:

- Players: explorers / captains / heroes of the portal world.
- Board: 4 elemental zones + central Great Portal + risky routes.
- Goal: collect 4 elemental keys and open the Great Portal.
- Tension: Catastrophe track.
- Excitement: chests, wrecked ships, portals, elemental die, missions, heroes.

Core loop:

```text
ROLL → CHOOSE ROUTE → COLLECT RESOURCE → RISK OR PLAY SAFE → OPEN PORTAL / COMPLETE MISSION → MOVE TOWARD FINAL PORTAL
```

## 3. Keep / remove

### Keep for v0.1

1. Four elements: Fire, Water, Air, Earth.
2. Elemental Sparks as the main collectible resource.
3. Crystals as rare resources.
4. Four elemental Keys as a simple victory target.
5. Central Great Portal.
6. Portals as route shortcuts with cost and risk.
7. Chests and wrecked ships as risk/reward spaces.
8. Hero helpers with simple abilities.
9. Artifacts as rare items.
10. Missions that force players to travel, not just loop.
11. Catastrophe as a shared timer.
12. Competitive and cooperative modes.
13. Studio evaluation checklist: Core Loop, Competition, Clarity, Tension, Reward, Risk, Variety, Interaction, Setup Time, Visual Hook.

### Remove or postpone

1. Personal 10-card player decks — too heavy for v0.1.
2. Seven separate decks — too much for family onboarding.
3. Complex conversion rules between every resource type.
4. Many curses, damages, states, and long status effects.
5. Bosses as a separate subsystem — keep as mission/test cards only.
6. Construction economy — not for the small family product.
7. Long penalties and skipped turns — bad for family pacing.

## 4. Product frame v0.1

Working title: **Portals: Elemental Sparks**.

- Type: family adventure race.
- Players: 2–4.
- Age: 8+.
- Playtime: 35–50 minutes.
- Weight: light / family-plus.
- Tone: bright, adventurous, kind, risky but not punitive.

Pitch:

Players travel through four elemental zones, collect Sparks, Crystals, Artifacts and Hero help, complete Missions, and try to obtain the four Elemental Keys to open the Great Portal before rivals or before the Catastrophe reaches its final stage.

## 5. Victory

### Competitive mode

The winner is the first player who:

1. Collects four different Elemental Keys: Fire, Water, Air, Earth.
2. Returns to the center.
3. Activates the Great Portal.

### Cooperative mode

Players collectively collect the four Elemental Keys and open the Great Portal before the Catastrophe track reaches 10.

## 6. Resources v0.1

Use six resource categories, but keep the first playtest physically simple.

1. Fire Spark — red.
2. Water Spark — blue.
3. Air Spark — white / light blue.
4. Earth Spark — green.
5. Crystal — transparent / violet universal rare resource.
6. Elemental Key — four unique tokens: Fire, Water, Air, Earth.

Simplified economy:

- Normal elemental space: gain 1 Spark of that zone.
- Spark space: gain 2 Sparks of that zone.
- 3 Sparks of the same element may be exchanged for 1 Crystal.
- Elemental Keys are earned through Gates, Missions, or rare Artifacts, not through dry exchange alone.

Do not make “4 different Crystals = 1 Key” the main rule in v0.1. A Key should feel like an event and an achievement.

## 7. Board space types v0.1

Keep ten space types, not twenty.

1. Normal elemental space — gain 1 Spark of the zone.
2. Spark space — gain 2 Sparks of the zone.
3. Crystal space — gain 1 Crystal.
4. Chest — draw Artifact/Treasure.
5. Wreck / ruins — roll the elemental die: find or danger.
6. Portal — move to a linked zone for a cost.
7. Elemental Gate — complete a requirement to gain a Key or major reward.
8. Hero helper — hire a Hero or receive a mini-quest.
9. Trap / risk — choose safe small effect or risk for a larger reward.
10. Catastrophe / event — advance Catastrophe or draw Catastrophe.

Speed-ups, secret passages, and bonus effects should mostly be cards, not extra board icons.

## 8. Dice

Use two dice.

### Movement die d6

Result = move 1–6 spaces.

Family rule: the player may stop before using the full roll if they stop on a meaningful space. This reduces frustration from overshooting.

### Elemental die d6

Faces:

1. Fire — fire effect.
2. Water — water effect.
3. Air — +1 movement or air transition.
4. Earth — find / place Crystal.
5. Portal — portal opens, closes, or changes link.
6. Catastrophe — advance Catastrophe or draw Catastrophe.

The elemental die is not rolled every turn. It is rolled when a player hits risk, wreck, portal, gate, or card instruction. This keeps chaos under control.

## 9. Decks v0.1

Reduce to five decks.

### 1. Artifacts / Treasures — 30 cards

Function: pleasant bonuses, items, chests, crystals.

Examples: Portal Compass, Wind Boots, Earth Shield, Water Lantern, Crystal Backpack.

### 2. Portals — 25 cards

Function: temporary routes, connection changes, unstable portals.

Examples: Random Rift, Elemental Jump, Unstable Portal, Double Portal.

### 3. Heroes — 20 cards

Function: simple helper abilities.

Examples: Fire Smith, Mermaid Guide, Air Courier, Stone Giant, Little Helper Spirit.

### 4. Missions — 20 cards

Function: reasons to move around the board instead of looping.

Examples: Repair the Wrecked Ship, Awaken the Fire Gate, Find the Sunken Crown, Rescue the Earth Hero.

### 5. Catastrophes — 12 cards

Function: shared timer and map disruption.

Examples: Eruption, Storm, Flood, Collapse, Elemental Chaos.

Postpone separate “Elements” and generic “Events” decks; distribute their effects into Portals, Missions, and Catastrophes.

## 10. Player cards

Do not use 10-card personal starter decks in v0.1.

Use:

- 1 character card.
- 1 player board.
- 3 Hero slots.
- 2 Artifact slots.
- Resource slots for Sparks, Crystals and Keys.

Characters v0.1:

1. Inventor — repairs ships/artifacts 1 resource cheaper.
2. Treasure Hunter — on Chest, draw 2 cards and keep 1.
3. Portal Mage — portals cost 1 Spark less.
4. Element Shaper — once per turn, convert 2 same Sparks into 1 any Spark.
5. Air Scout — after moving, may move +1 space.
6. Earth Keeper — ignores first Trap each round.

## 11. Player turn v0.1

1. Roll movement die.
2. Move 1–6 spaces; may stop early.
3. Resolve the space.
4. Optionally play 1 Artifact / Hero / Portal card.
5. Optionally complete 1 Mission if conditions are met.
6. If Catastrophe was triggered, advance the track or draw a Catastrophe card.

Target turn length: 60–90 seconds per player.

## 12. Board v0.1

Recommended board structure:

- 4 elemental zones with 12–16 spaces each.
- Central Great Portal.
- 4 Elemental Gates.
- 8 portal spaces.
- 8–12 chests.
- 4–6 wrecks / ruins.
- 4–8 Hero spaces.
- 6–8 traps / risks.
- 4 Catastrophe spaces.
- area for 5 decks.
- Catastrophe track 0–10.
- area for 3 open Missions.

## 13. Studio prototype checklist

| Criterion | Question | Goal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Core Loop | Does the player make an interesting decision every turn? | Not just roll-and-move |
| Competition | Is there competition for spaces, cards, or routes? | Excitement |
| Clarity | Can the board be understood in 2 minutes? | Family accessibility |
| Tension | Is there pressure from time or rivals? | Dynamism |
| Reward | Does the player often receive pleasant bonuses? | Joy |
| Risk | Can the player risk something for a bigger reward? | Emotion |
| Variety | Do sessions differ from each other? | Replayability |
| Interaction | Do players affect one another? | Liveliness |
| Setup Time | Can it be set up quickly? | Mass market fit |
| Visual Hook | Does the photo make people want to buy it? | Sales |

## 14. Playtest success targets

Prototype v0.1 is alive if:

1. First turn is explained in 5 minutes.
2. Players make choices from turn two.
3. Each player receives a reward at least every 2 turns.
4. At least 3 portals are used during a session.
5. At least 1 player risks on a wreck/chest/portal.
6. Catastrophe creates pressure without destroying fun.
7. Session ends in 35–50 minutes.
8. Each player remembers one cool moment after the game.
9. Children understand the goal without lore explanation.
10. Adults are not bored while playing with children.

## 15. Minimum components v0.1

- 1 board.

- 6 character cards.
- 4 Spark types, about 20 tokens each.
- 12 universal Crystals.
- 4 Elemental Keys.
- 1 movement d6.
- 1 elemental d6.
- 30 Artifact/Treasure cards.
- 25 Portal cards.
- 20 Hero cards.
- 20 Mission cards.
- 12 Catastrophe cards.
- 1 Catastrophe track 0–10.
- 4 player boards.

Approximate card count: 107 cards + tokens + board. Enough for a product-like prototype, not overloaded.

## 17. Final working formula

**Portals: Elemental Sparks** is a small family adventure race where players collect Sparks, Crystals, Heroes and Artifacts, complete Missions, risk on Chests and Wrecks, use Portals as route shortcuts, and try to open the Great Portal before the Catastrophe catches up.
