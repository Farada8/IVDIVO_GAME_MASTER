## 2. Short Pitch

IVDIVO is a cooperative campaign board game where every crisis has a hidden cause.

A monster is only a Manifestation. A curse is only a Distortion. A collapsing city, a broken portal, a haunted law of reality — all of them grow from a Root.

Your team of awakened explorers must travel through a modular map of worlds, build personal decks, open and survive portals, investigate Reality Fractures, and choose how each scenario ends. Do you perform a Rupture — cutting the threat away quickly, at a cost? Or do you attempt Transformation — harder, slower, and riskier, but able to change the world permanently?

No app. No hidden digital content. The story lives in cards, maps, scenario sheets, campaign memory, and the decisions your group makes at the table.

Russian comment: pitch строится вокруг понятной формулы: не просто победить монстра, а найти причину. Это делает игру отличимой от обычного dungeon crawler и от обычной campaign adventure.

## 3. Why This Game Is Different

**Most campaign games ask: can you survive the threat? IVDIVO asks: what is producing the threat?**

IVDIVO Core Box is built around five linked design pillars:

1. **Root-Cause Adventure** — each scenario has visible danger, distorted rules, and a hidden Root that explains why the crisis keeps returning.
2. **Rupture vs Transformation** — every major resolution is not just win/lose, but a strategic choice between cutting the problem away and changing the system that created it.
3. **Portals as Gameplay, Not Decoration** — portals are risky routes, timing windows, economy gates, and story consequences, not just map shortcuts.
4. **Deckbuilding with Identity** — your character deck grows from actions, scars, tools, forms, and role choices; new cards often replace old ones rather than bloating the deck forever.
5. **A Mythic Cosmology Map** — worlds are not only locations; they change what actions mean, what resources matter, and what kind of truth can be discovered.

The goal is not to make the biggest box possible. The goal is to create a finished Core Box that feels complete on its own, then can expand naturally into new worlds, orbitals, characters, campaigns, and deluxe components.

Russian comment: здесь аккуратно соединены game design pillars. Формулировка «not the biggest box possible» важна для доверия: Kickstarter-аудитория устала от коробок, которые обещают больше, чем могут протестировать.

## 4. How to Play in 5 Steps

### Step 1 — Choose a Character and a Role

Pick one of 8 characters, each with a personal history, starting deck, fear, mistake, and transformation path. Then choose or assign one of 5 team roles that defines how your character contributes to the mission: scouting, guarding, revealing, shaping, or harmonising the world state.

### Step 2 — Build the Scenario Map

Set up a modular world map using location tiles, portals, fracture markers, scenario cards, and the current world law. The map changes from scenario to scenario: routes, risks, allies, and Root clues appear in different configurations.

### Step 3 — Explore, Build, and Upgrade Your Deck

On your turn, move, investigate, protect, trade, stabilise portals, resolve encounters, gain cards, and improve your deck. You are not simply collecting power. You are deciding what kind of person your character becomes under pressure.

### Step 4 — Face the Reality Fracture

Each scenario escalates through a visible Manifestation, a deeper Distortion, and a hidden Root. Fighting the surface threat helps, but if you never discover the Root, the crisis may return in another form.

### Step 5 — Choose Rupture or Transformation

At the end of the scenario, the team chooses how to resolve the Root. Rupture is faster and cleaner, but can leave scars. Transformation can create a permanent improvement, but requires deeper costs, more risk, and better preparation. Either way, the world remembers.

## 6. Characters

IVDIVO characters are not just stat blocks. Each one has:

- a practical role in the team;
- a mistake from the past;
- a fear that can distort decisions;
- a personal deckbuilding direction;
- a relationship to portals and world laws;
- a possible path of Rupture, collapse, or Transformation.

The Core Box is designed around 8 characters. Working archetypal examples:

1. **The Scout** — reads unstable routes, finds hidden paths, and risks going too far ahead.
2. **The Guardian** — protects the team and key locations, but must learn when to release control.
3. **The Seer** — reveals Distortions and future consequences, but can mistake one truth for the whole truth.
4. **The Portal Guide** — survives unstable transitions and turns dangerous portals into strategic opportunities.
5. **The Builder** — restores damaged structures, creates safe nodes, and shapes the modular map.
6. **The Flamebearer** — can burn away corruption, but risks destroying what might have been transformed.
7. **The Mediator** — negotiates with inhabitants, allies, and hostile powers that are not simply enemies.
8. **The Synthesist** — connects separate systems, but must balance unity against over-control.

The 5 role system lets the same character play differently across campaigns. A Scout as a Guardian feels different from a Scout as a Seer. A Flamebearer in a stabilising role creates a different story than a Flamebearer focused on rapid Rupture.

## 7. Worlds

The Core Box begins inside a cosmology of five vertical worlds and multiple orbital realms. These are not just lore chapters — each world changes how the game works.

### The Waking World
Bodies, buildings, roads, resources, visible damage, and immediate survival. Here you build, repair, move, protect, and pay the concrete price of every decision.

### The Veil World
Hidden motives, echoes, false images, emotional residues, and invisible causes. Here you reveal what the surface cannot explain.

### The Great Network
Systems, routes, institutions, oaths, permissions, and large-scale connections. Here one decision can change how whole regions, factions, or portals behave.

### The Fire World
Purification, burning pressure, irreversible change, and the destruction of dead forms. Here transformation becomes possible — but never cheap.

### The Synthesis Horizon
The highest layer of connection, where separate worlds, roles, tools, and consequences can be joined into a new configuration.

Around these worlds move orbital realms: dangerous lower realms, radiant realms, fae-like territories, angelic civilizations, archetypal regions, and other realities that can intersect the campaign through portals, events, factions, and expansions.

## 8. Portals

Portals are one of the central systems of IVDIVO.

A portal can be:

- a route into another world;
- a temporary timing window;
- a damaged seam in reality;
- a faction-controlled passage;
- a risk that must be stabilised;
- a strategic shortcut that may create long-term consequences.

You do not open portals for flavour. You open them because the Root may be unreachable from the surface map. You cross them because the team needs resources, allies, clues, or a different law of reality. You fear them because every transition can change the board, the deck, or the future campaign setup.

Some portals are stable. Some are infected. Some rotate, drift, or align only at the right moment. Some portals do not merely move you to another place — they change the rules of what your actions mean.

Russian comment: порталы — ключевой коммерческий крючок. Текст объясняет, что портал не просто «дверь на другую карту», а экономический, пространственный, сюжетный и риск-механизм.

## 9. Root-Cause System

Every major crisis in IVDIVO has three layers:

### 1. Manifestation

The visible problem: a creature, collapse, haunting, plague, invasion, broken machine, social panic, or hostile event. You can fight it, contain it, delay it, or survive it.

### 2. Distortion

The rule beneath the problem: something has changed the logic of the world. Maybe trust decays faster. Maybe portals mislead. Maybe saved inhabitants become marked. Maybe a safe route now demands a price.

### 3. Root

The cause that keeps producing the crisis. The Root is not always evil in a simple way. It may be a broken oath, an abandoned system, a corrupted law, a forgotten bargain, a wounded place, or a power that was forced into a monstrous form.

If you destroy only the Manifestation, the scenario may be won temporarily. If you understand the Distortion, you can control the danger. If you face the Root, you decide what kind of ending the campaign will remember.

Russian comment: это центральная формула страницы. Она превращает игру из «убей монстра» в «разберись с причиной». Это сильное позиционирование для Kickstarter.

## 10. Rupture vs Transformation

At the heart of the campaign is a recurring choice:

**Rupture** means cutting the Root away. It is decisive, often faster, and sometimes necessary. But Rupture can leave scars: damaged portals, abandoned allies, unstable zones, faction distrust, or new dangers in later scenarios.

**Transformation** means changing the conditions that produced the Root. It is harder. It may require more clues, more resources, more risk, and more coordination. But it can create transformed zones, new allies, restored routes, and permanent campaign advantages.

Neither option is the “correct moral answer.” A desperate Rupture may save lives. A poorly prepared Transformation may fail catastrophically. The right decision depends on your characters, your deck, the world state, the Root, and the price your team is willing to carry.

Russian comment: важно не делать Преобразование «доброй концовкой», а Разрыв «плохой концовкой». Оба варианта должны быть стратегически разумными. В плейтестах целевой коридор выбора — чтобы ни одна ветка не доминировала.

## 11. Campaign

The Core Box campaign is planned as a 10–12 scenario arc, with additional scenarios in the design bank for testing, replacement, and stretch goals.

The campaign begins with local survival pressure and grows into a cosmic investigation. Early scenarios teach characters, map movement, portals, and the first Reality Fractures. Mid-campaign scenarios introduce deeper Distortions, faction consequences, orbital interventions, and harder Root choices. Late scenarios move toward Fire-level transformation, systemic consequences, and a Synthesis-level final decision.

The campaign is designed as replayable, not destructive legacy. You may mark a campaign log, add memory cards, unlock routes, scar zones, transform locations, and alter future setup — without permanently destroying the game.

## 12. Replayability

IVDIVO is built to be replayed in multiple ways:

- 8 characters with different personal arcs.
- 5 roles that reshape how a character contributes.
- Modular world maps and variable portal networks.
- Different Reality Fractures and Root combinations.
- Multiple paths to scenario resolution.
- Rupture and Transformation outcomes that change future setup.
- Campaign memory cards: consequences, scars, and transformed zones.
- Orbitals and faction interactions that can enter the campaign in different patterns.
- Deckbuilding choices that replace, refine, or redirect your strategy.

A replay is not just “try the same fight again.” It is a different investigation into why reality is breaking — and a different answer to what your group is willing to change.

## 15. Production Plan

IVDIVO Core Box is a card-heavy campaign game, so production will focus on readability, component sorting, card quality, scenario clarity, and manufacturing reliability.

1. **Prototype Completion** — finish the playable vertical slice: characters, roles, map, portals, Reality Fracture, Root resolution, and first campaign memory cards.
2. **Internal and Guided Playtesting** — test rules explanation, playtime, portal clarity, Root importance, Rupture / Transformation balance, downtime, and replay desire.
3. **Blind Playtesting Gate** — the game must be playable without the designer at the table.
4. **Art and Graphic Design Lock** — final art, icons, card templates, map visuals, and packaging lock only after gameplay structure is stable.
5. **Manufacturing Quotes and Pre-Press** — final component count, card stock, insert, box size, punchboards, tokens, rulebook, and shipping cartons are quoted before public launch or disclosed as pending.
6. **Kickstarter Fulfilment** — production updates focus on actual milestones: files prepared, proof copies, mass production, freight, fulfilment partner handoff, and regional shipping.

## 16. Playtest Proof

We are not asking you to trust a promise of depth. We are building a test process to prove it.

The current test protocol checks explanation time, first meaningful choice, total playtime, clarity of turn structure, interest of decisions, portal function, Reality Fracture pressure, Distortion clarity, Root importance, Rupture / Transformation balance, downtime, alpha-player risk, and desire to replay.

Our launch gate is simple: if players cannot understand the Root system, if portals feel like ordinary movement, if one final choice dominates, or if the game cannot be played without the designer explaining it, the campaign is not ready.

The target path is internal tests, guided external tests, and blind playtests before final campaign launch. We will share prototype reports, changes, and lessons openly as the campaign page develops.

## 17. Risks and Honesty

IVDIVO is ambitious. That is exciting, but it also creates real risks.

### Risk: Too Much Game
A 450–550 card campaign box can become bloated if every idea enters the Core Box. Control: the Core Box must stay focused on the playable loop — deckbuilding, modular map, portals, Reality Fractures, Root resolution, and campaign memory.

### Risk: Complexity
A mythic cosmology game can become hard to explain. Control: the first game experience must teach through play, not through lore lectures.

### Risk: Campaign Balance
Rupture and Transformation must both remain valid. If one becomes the obvious answer, the central choice fails. Control: track choice frequency and redesign costs until both paths are genuinely playable.

### Risk: Production Cost
Card count, map components, tokens, inserts, and premium upgrades can raise manufacturing and shipping costs. Control: quote the Core Box before locking prices and keep deluxe upgrades optional.

### Risk: Lore Overload
IVDIVO has a large internal cosmology. Control: the public game must stand on its own as an independent IP. The Core Box will introduce only what the gameplay needs.

### Risk: No App Required
No app reduces digital dependency, but it means the physical system must carry the full campaign logic clearly. Control: player aids, campaign memory cards, scenario structure, and component organisation must be tested before launch.

We would rather delay the campaign than ship a game that is beautiful but unclear, huge but unfocused, or deep only on paper.

Russian comment: этот блок повышает доверие. Kickstarter-аудитория ценит честность: не скрывать риски сложности, производства, баланса и lore overload.
