# PORTALS_OF_STARS_RESOURCE_ECONOMY_v0.1_DRAFT

Status: DRAFT / PLAYTEST REQUIRED
Project: GAME_01_PORTALS_OF_STARS
Date: 2026-07-23

## 1. Design target

Game: «Порталы» / «Порталы Звёзд»
Players: 2–5
Time: 30–40 minutes
Weight target: 1.8–2.0
Resource system: 4 typed Sparks + 1 Joker Spark
Reference feel: Splendor + Century: Spice Road + Quacks-style bag risk

Core economic idea:
Players collect Sparks, convert them into portal openings, and score Light through route objectives. The system must feel as clear as Splendor, as tempo-driven as Century: Spice Road, and as emotionally risky as Quacks, while staying family-friendly.

## 2. Spark types

| Type | Color | Role | Economy behavior |
|---|---|---|---|
| Earth Spark | Green | Stable/basic routes | Cheapest and most common |
| Water Spark | Blue | Movement/flexible paths | Medium demand, route control |
| Fire Spark | Red | Risk/strong portals | High payoff, higher trap pressure |
| Air Spark | White/Cyan | Speed/teleport paths | Efficient but often asymmetric |
| Star Spark | Gold/Violet | Joker | Rare universal substitute |

## 3. Bank and supply

| Item | Number | Reason |
|---|---:|---|
| Earth Sparks | 24 | Most common; supports early actions |
| Water Sparks | 24 | Main movement/resource color |
| Fire Sparks | 24 | Enough for all players, but still contested |
| Air Sparks | 24 | Supports routes and portal jumps |
| Star/Joker Sparks | 12 | Rare; about 11% of physical Spark bank |
| Total Spark tokens | 108 | Enough for 5 players without constant shortage |

## 4. Player storage limits

| Limit | Value | Reason |
|---|---:|---|
| Normal hand/resource limit | 8 Sparks | Below Century's 10-cube feel to keep family tempo fast |
| Temporary overflow | 10 Sparks until end of turn | Allows exciting combos without hoarding |
| Star/Joker holding limit | 2 | Prevents joker hoarding and runaway flexibility |

## 5. Inflow per turn

| Source | Inflow | Reason |
|---|---:|---|
| Basic take action | Take 2 Sparks from market/available spaces | Slower than Splendor's 3-token burst because movement also gives value |
| Cell reward | +1 Spark when stopping on Spark cell | Makes movement meaningful |
| Chest / Ruin | Draw bag token or card; average +1 to +2 value | Adds Quacks-like push-your-luck |
| Portal income | Owner gets +1 Spark when another player uses their portal | Soft interaction, no blocking |
| Event bonus | Usually +1 Spark to all or selected trailing player | Avoids punishing weaker players |

## 6. Portal cost tiers

| Tier | Cost | Light points | Expected timing | Design comparison |
|---|---|---:|---|---|
| Tier 1: Local Portal | 2 same Sparks OR 3 any | 2 | Turns 2–4 | Equivalent to early Splendor low-cost card: quick conversion into engine/points |
| Tier 2: World Portal | 2 same + 1 different OR 4 any | 4 | Turns 5–8 | Comparable to Century mid-order: requires planning and conversion |
| Tier 3: Star Portal | 3 different + 1 Star OR 5 with at least 3 types | 6 | Turns 9–12 | Late swing, but not enough alone to win |
| Tier 4: Central Portal | 1 of each type + 1 Star | 8 | Endgame objective | Prestige-like finisher; should trigger final tempo pressure |

## 7. Match tempo

| Parameter | Value | Reason |
|---|---:|---|
| Target final score | 30 Light | About two times Splendor's 15-point trigger because more points come from map actions |
| End trigger A | A player reaches 30 Light | Clear family-friendly race endpoint |
| End trigger B | 12 portals opened in 2–3p, 14 in 4p, 16 in 5p | Scales with player count |
| End trigger C | Event deck empties after 18 events | Caps long games |
| Expected turns/player | 12–14 | Keeps 30–40 minute target |
| Expected total rounds | 10–12 in 5p; 12–14 in 2–3p | Higher player count needs fewer personal turns |
| Average Light per player turn | 2.1–2.5 after turn 4 | Needed to reach 30 by turn 12–14 |

## 8. Bag instead of die

Each player has a personal bag. The bag replaces the die for risk spaces, ruins, shipwrecks, traps, and optional boosted movement.

### Starting bag

| Token | Count | Effect |
|---|---:|---|
| Earth Spark token | 2 | Gain 1 Earth |
| Water Spark token | 2 | Gain 1 Water |
| Fire Spark token | 2 | Gain 1 Fire |
| Air Spark token | 2 | Gain 1 Air |
| Star Spark token | 1 | Gain 1 Star or count as any type |
| Step token | 2 | Move +1 or ignore 1 difficult cell |
| Chest token | 1 | Draw Treasure card |
| Trap token | 2 | Trigger trap consequence |
| Total | 14 | Trap chance starts at about 14% per draw |

### Trap result

| Situation | Effect |
|---|---|
| First trap this turn | Stop drawing; choose: lose 1 Spark OR move back 1 cell |
| Second trap in same turn | Stop drawing; lose 1 Spark and do not activate the destination bonus |
| Trap protection | Spend 1 matching Spark or use a Hero ability to cancel |
| Comeback protection | If you are last on the Light track, first trap each round only stops drawing, no loss |

### Anti-randomness controls

| Control | Rule | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| Player choice | Drawing from bag is optional except on trap cells | Risk belongs to the player |
| Known bag state | Players may inspect their own discard pile anytime | Similar to Quacks memory/odds management |
| Bag building | Some cards add good tokens; traps add temporary bad tokens only | Strategy changes odds over time |
| Stop point | Player may stop after each non-trap draw | Greed creates emotion without pure chaos |
| Compensation | Bad draws create minor setbacks, not turn loss | Maintains family tone |
| Catch-up guard | Last player has softer first trap | No beating the losing player |

## 10. Open playtest questions

1. Does 2-Spark Tier 1 portal open too quickly?
2. Is 30 Light too high for families under 40 minutes?
3. Does Star/Joker feel exciting or too strong?
4. Does bag trap chance create laughter rather than frustration?
5. Does portal-income create enough interaction without blocking?
6. Is the 8-Spark storage limit too tight for younger players?
