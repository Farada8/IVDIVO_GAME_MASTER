## 1. Founder question

```text
What are the chances of raising $30k-$50k on Kickstarter for the Portals board game?
```

## 2. Direct answer

Raising $30k-$50k is realistic, but not automatic.

The target is achievable if the project becomes a clear family adventure product with:

```text
- strong table presence;
- short playtestable rules;
- real prototype photos/video;
- clear family positioning;
- attractive portal/spark mechanic;
- visible character identity;
- reasonable pledge price;
- pre-launch audience;
- reviewers/previewers;
- manufacturing and fulfillment plan.
```

Without audience, playtest proof, rulebook, prototype video, and campaign preparation, the chances are low even if the art is strong.

## 3. Probability estimate

### Current state today

```text
Chance to raise $30k-$50k: about 10-20%.
```

Reason: the visual direction is promising, but the playable product, audience, rules, manufacturing plan, and campaign assets are not yet complete.

### After building a playable prototype

```text
Chance to raise $30k-$50k: about 30-45%.
```

Required:

```text
- 2-page quick rules;
- 20-30 minute family demo;
- 3-ring board;
- Spark economy;
- 6 character cards;
- portal market row;
- 80-120 cards;
- playtest logs;
- prototype photos;
- landing page;
- 500-1500 qualified email followers.
```

### After serious prelaunch marketing

```text
Chance to raise $30k-$50k: about 50-70%.
```

Required:

```text
- 1500-3000 qualified followers/email leads;
- 100+ playtesters or demo players;
- 5-10 preview/reviewer contacts;
- clear pledge level around $39-$69;
- professional prototype video;
- manufacturing quotes;
- shipping estimates;
- Kickstarter page built around family fantasy adventure, not abstract metaphysics.
```

### Best-case prepared launch

```text
Chance to raise $50k+: about 70%+ only if the audience is already warm.
```

This requires a real campaign funnel before launch, not only a beautiful board.

## 4. Main equation

A $30k-$50k campaign likely needs roughly:

```text
600 backers x $50 average pledge = $30,000
800 backers x $62 average pledge = about $50,000
1000 backers x $50 average pledge = $50,000
```

Therefore the project needs not only art, but a system capable of converting several hundred real buyers.

## 5. Product positioning required

The strongest public positioning is:

```text
Portals: a colorful family fantasy adventure board game where players collect magical Sparks, open portals, race through three rings of adventure, recruit heroes, face creatures, and unlock the Great Portal.
```

Avoid selling it publicly as:

```text
complex metaphysical orbital ontology;
abstract esoteric system;
heavy philosophical board game;
unclear IP universe without simple table fun.
```

Internal IVDIVO canon can remain deep, but the Kickstarter page must be instantly understandable.

## 6. Why the current art helps

The generated direction has:

```text
- bright circular board;
- portals as a visible hook;
- chests, shipwrecks, heroes, monsters;
- card decks;
- dice;
- player pawns;
- colorful family energy.
```

This gives good table presence and image appeal.

However, art alone does not fund a campaign. The campaign needs a playable loop:

```text
Move -> choose -> risk -> collect Sparks -> play cards -> open portals -> race to inner source.
```

## 7. Minimum campaign package for $30k-$50k

```text
1. Game title and clear promise.
2. 3-ring board prototype.
3. 6 characters.
4. Spark tokens in 6 colors.
5. Portal market row of 5 cards.
6. 80-120 playable cards.
7. 2-page quick rules.
8. Full rulebook draft.
9. 5-10 playtest reports.
10. Campaign video.
11. Prototype photos.
12. Manufacturing quote.
13. Shipping table.
14. 500-3000 email leads depending on target.
15. Reviewer/previewer outreach.
```

## 8. Main risks

```text
- Too many systems at once.
- Board looks exciting but gameplay is roll-and-move.
- No clear victory condition.
- Too much IVDIVO terminology for family market.
- Manufacturing too expensive.
- No prelaunch audience.
- No rulebook.
- No credible delivery plan.
```

## 9. Recommended target

For first launch:

```text
Funding goal: $20k-$30k.
Internal target: $30k-$50k.
Dream target: $100k+ if prelaunch list grows.
```

A lower visible goal can create faster early success, but must still cover manufacturing and fulfillment safely.
